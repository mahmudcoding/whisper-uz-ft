================================================================================
1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The project builds a dedicated open-weight Uzbek ASR system based on OpenAI Whisper large-v3. The primary objective is minimizing Uzbek WER/CER. Preservation of multilingual Whisper behavior is not a goal. Catastrophic forgetting of English, Russian, Turkish, or other languages is acceptable when it improves Uzbek. A separate model can be used for other languages.

The owner repeatedly emphasized that quality matters more than multilingual preservation, and that the ASR target is Uzbek-only offline enterprise transcription for meetings, calls, webinars, podcasts, noisy real-world speech, and similar production workloads. Uzbek Latin output is preferred; mixed Uzbek Latin/Cyrillic and Russian/English code-switching remain relevant data/evaluation concerns, but training optimization is Uzbek quality.

Base model: openai/whisper-large-v3. Main comparison baseline from outside project: rubaiSTT v2 medium and Kotib/uzbek_stt_v1. The rubaistt_v2 repository was supplied at /home/mahmud/rubaistt_v2-open-sourced and treated as reference material for Uzbek ASR best practices. It showed that their strength is data scale/diversity and filtering rather than uniquely superior training code.

Important high-level conclusions reached over time:
- Raw Whisper large-v3 performs badly on Uzbek because multilingual priors often produce Turkish/Kazakh-like output. Raw baseline recorded earlier: WER 1.0522, CER 0.4590.
- A mini fine-tune gave large improvement: WER 0.4961, CER 0.1094.
- Full fine-tuning on only USC degraded relative to partial FT. This changed strategy away from naive full FT.
- Lower encoder layers likely contain valuable universal acoustic features and should not be aggressively updated on small corpora. Decoder and upper/mid encoder adaptation are more promising.
- Gold corpus expansion from USC-only to USC + Common Voice + FLEURS reached 207.115h. FeruzaSpeech was moved out of Gold into train-only Silver because of restrictive/gated terms.
- LR-search on 30h proxy strongly improved over earlier conservative settings. The best 30h proxy WER so far is from encoder B/C/D + decoder all at 2e-5: WER 19.134%, CER 4.844% on validation proxy. A B=1e-5, C/D/decoder=2e-5 run closely tied WER and slightly tied CER at step 1600: WER 19.274%, CER 4.826%.

5. TECHNICAL DECISIONS AND RATIONALE
================================================================================

Uzbek-only objective:
- Decision: optimize Uzbek WER/CER only; multilingual forgetting acceptable.
- Rationale: raw multilingual Whisper often confuses Uzbek with Turkish/Kazakh, harming quality. Forced Uzbek and Uzbek-specific adaptation improve the actual business goal.
- Risk: non-Uzbek ASR degrades. This is acceptable.

Forced Uzbek decoding:
- language=uz, task=transcribe in training/evaluation/inference. Automatic language detection is not trusted for Uzbek.
- Generation uses num_beams=1 in most training/search configs unless evaluating alternatives.

Protected partial-FT baseline:
- models/partial_ft_usc_baseline/ is immutable. It has current best completed full-test model on USC-only baseline: WER 20.05%, CER 5.29%.
- It must not be overwritten, moved, or deleted.

Full FT is not default:
- Initially full FT was considered because Uzbek-only catastrophic forgetting was allowed and rubaiSTT used full FT.
- Measured USC full FT with all 1.543B params, BF16, encoder LR 2e-6, decoder LR 8e-6, 1 epoch degraded to WER 22.22%, CER 5.66 versus partial FT 20.05/5.29.
- Conclusion: full FT on small USC can damage useful acoustic features. Search should focus on decoder, upper encoder, and mid encoder, not entire encoder by default.

Layer-wise/blockwise LR:
- Motivation: decoder language prior likely dominant; upper/mid encoder may need adaptation; lower encoder likely useful and fragile.
- Supported groups: encoder A=0-7, B=8-15, C=16-23, D=24-31, decoder 0-31.
- Current best proxy evidence suggests training B/C/D + decoder at 2e-5 is strong; full encoder at 2e-5 collapses.

Validation/test integrity:
- LR-search uses train for training and val for checkpoint selection only. Test is not loaded/evaluated during search.
- Configs enforce load_test_split:false and evaluate_test_after_training:false for search.
- data_leakage_audit.md passed. Path overlap 0; transcript collisions exist but are not treated as leakage for read speech when audio/speakers are disjoint.

BF16:
- A40 supports BF16. New large-v3 training/search uses BF16 unless measured otherwise.

Scheduler:
- LR-search uses HuggingFace/Transformers scheduler configured by YAML. Most current configs use scheduler: cosine with warmup_ratio inherited from base configs. Config LR values are peak/max LR after warmup, followed by cosine decay. Observed LR in high LR run: warmup to 5e-5 around early steps then decayed near zero by final step. This matters: reported LR is not constant.

Speed optimization:
- The owner asked why GPU/VRAM were underutilized. For proxy runs, gradient checkpointing was disabled, batch size increased to 4, duration bucketing enabled, gradient accumulation adjusted to 8 to preserve approximate effective batch 32.
- Batch4 fast partial encoder runs used around 36 GB peak VRAM and ~2h40m per 30h proxy run. Batch6/8 benchmarks were explored; batch4 became safe standard for B/C/D and C/D runs. Full encoder batch2 OOMed; full encoder uses batch1 safe.

Teacher model for Silver scoring:
- Original/accidental idea of using own finetuned model as teacher was rejected by owner as wrong.
- Kotib/uzbek_stt_v1 selected as teacher. Config pins Kotib revision 0e239511f65c1c7bbf426619a1ee9ea628411344, faster-whisper/CTranslate2 FP16, CUDA, forced Uzbek, beam1.

FeruzaSpeech governance:
- FeruzaSpeech was acquired and prepared but then moved from Gold to train-only Silver due to gated/restrictive terms. It remains useful but is not part of high-trust Gold evaluation/training baseline.

12. FAILURES, REJECTED PATHS, AND LESSONS
================================================================================

Rejected or failed paths:
- Raw Whisper large-v3 without Uzbek forcing/adaptation is not usable enough; Turkish/Kazakh confusion.
- Full FT on USC degraded WER/CER versus partial FT.
- Full encoder 2e-5 on 30h proxy collapsed with huge hallucination.
- Full all-block aggressive batch2 OOMed during AdamW state initialization. Full encoder requires batch1 or different memory plan.
- Decoder-only high LR can hallucinate badly. phase2_decoder_2em05 and phase1a/1b high LR decoder-only had extreme WER/CER and hallucination in some runs.
- C=1e-5, D/decoder=5e-5 had competitive WER but bad CER; WER alone can hide character-level degradation.
- Using own finetuned model as Silver teacher was rejected; Kotib teacher selected.
- Beam5 long-form inference unguarded was skipped/aborted; needs VAD/max token/hallucination guardrails.
- Docs/state.json can be stale during fast experiment changes. Runtime logs/metrics are authoritative when checking current status.

Operational mistakes/lessons:
- Checkpoint/final_model artifacts in old LR-search outputs consumed lots of disk; metrics/configs/logs are more important for search history than all intermediate weights.
- `docs/FINAL_RECOMMENDATION` can lag behind later Phase4x results. As of export, older final recommendation selects encoder_16_31 + decoder at dec8e-6/enc5e-6, but later evidence favors B/C/D + decoder at 2e-5.
- Do not run duplicate controllers or duplicate jobs against same output directory.
- Before stopping processes, inspect tmux/PIDs/output dir to avoid killing active desired job.
- Test metrics must be forbidden in LR-search comparison.

17. RUBAISTT REFERENCE FINDINGS
================================================================================

The rubaistt_v2 repository was deeply mined as Uzbek ASR reference material. Important findings recorded from that analysis:
- They use Whisper Medium, not large-v3.
- They use full fine-tuning, BF16, around 4 epochs.
- They use strong text normalization.
- They use pseudo-labeling with Gemini.
- They filter corpus based on similarity/WER.
- Their main advantage is larger and more diverse data (~500h) rather than a fundamentally better training stack.
- Their dataset diversity includes podcasts, news, conversational audio, dialect speech.
- Useful ideas: aggressive normalization, teacher/pseudo-label agreement filtering, diverse domains, dataset filtering, and pseudo-label expansion.
- Their choices are not automatically optimal for this large-v3 pipeline; full FT was empirically worse on our smaller USC corpus.
